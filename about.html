What To Know About Self Driving Cars

Self-driving cars, also known as autonomous or driverless cars, are vehicles that use sensors, cameras, artificial intelligence (AI), and other technologies to navigate without a human driver.

How They Work

Self-driving cars use sensors to create a 3D map of their surroundings, including other vehicles, pedestrians, traffic lights, and road signs. The car's computer system then processes this data to make decisions about steering, acceleration, braking, and cruising speed.

Benifits

Self-driving cars could potentially reduce traffic congestion, lower the number of accidents, and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

Levels of automation

SAE International defines five levels of automation, from Level 0 to Level 5, with Level 5 being full automation. Most vehicles on the road today are Level 2, which means they can operate themselves but the driver is still responsible for paying attention and taking back control if needed.
